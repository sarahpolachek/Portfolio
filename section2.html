Sherpa 6 - UX Process

My work at Sherpa 6 as a UX/UI Engineer is protected and covered by a Secret Security Clearance. I am unable to speak upon or display any of my work. However, below is an overview of the design processes and methodologies that I have worked through while on a project. From understanding the product, researching needs, and analyzing data to designing wireframes and testing iterations, the process my team and I utilized for this project rapidly progressed through each stage with a deadline of three sprints, six weeks, to present an Alpha demo to our stakeholders. The development of this product will continue past our alpha demo, to refine the process and product we develop as well as to add more functionality.

UX Process and Methodologies


Understanding and Defining the Product


Prior to designing, I needed to take the time to understand this product and its needs. Asking the question of who our user is, what problem are we trying solve and why we are the ones that can help them solve problem? What answers do we hold?


During this phase, we were able to have discussions with stakeholder which played a key role in gathering insights about goals and the high-level needs. Using value mapping, we defined what the product is, who will use it, and why they need it. This aided in building a strong consensus between teams and stakeholders around how the product will match user and business needs.

Product Research


In the following stage, the UX team moved into the research phase. This phase focused on user research as well as dissecting and analyzing a legacy app that had once been prototyped but never fully implemented. This legacy app was developed with a very similar concept to the application we were building presently, however, the app never had the capabilities to be used in the field.


By completing usability testing on the legacy app and analyzing the core functionality, we were able to develop an initial overview of necessary functionality that the new application should consider implementing. This list helped us in brainstorming new ways to capture the concept of the application and how we could best design and implement the necessary functionality.


Our user research also included focus groups of 3-5 target users, where we were able to discuss real-world scenarios including current frustrations with how the process was currently being accomplished and what would make their job easier. These focus groups encouraged natural dialogue and conversation between the focus group, the UX team, and the software team.

Analysis


During the analysis phase, the goal was to move to understanding why the user wants, needs, or thinks about the product.


This phase included developing user personas. To note though, it was difficult to speak directly with our users since communication is limited with them directly. We were, however, able to develop three main user personas based upon seniority. These personas covered each of the users job responsibilities, goals, and frustrations.


Creating the user personas in turned helped us develop user stories to further understand how each user would use the product. We were also able to utilize focus group sessions to develop a storyboard to connect a user persona to their stories about how the product could be used from start to finish.

Design


The design phased pulled together stakeholdersâ€™ expectations and research of the users wants and needs as well as user personas and stories. This is the phase where we really begin to piece together the flow screen by screen.


The main part of this phase involved creating user flows which worked through each step required in order for the user to have a rewarding experience. I then designed low-fidelity wireframes to help visualize the user flow and create the main structure of the application which included placement of elements and button as well as text placeholders.


Following the approval of the wireframes, I began developing the high-fidelity prototypes. These prototypes not only focused on the main interaction and experience but they also included exact pixels and dp for developers to reference when programming. During this phase, I also created a design system of components and styles (primary and secondary colors, font type, font sizes, icons, and buttons) as well as design specifications which included a main icon package for use while programming.

Validation (Testing)


The next phase in our process will be to complete validation testing. This step will help us understand whether the design really works for our users. This testing will also help validate the product for both stakeholders and end users.


Although we are still in the design phase of our products development and have more sprints to complete before we are ready for testing, our testing sessions will be with our focus group, who represents our target audience. We plan on beta testing this product as well. During any testing session, we will also be paying close attention to the clicks, navigation time, and searches while the app is in use as well as the time it takes to complete main tasks.
